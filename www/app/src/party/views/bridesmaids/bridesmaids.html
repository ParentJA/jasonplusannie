Maid of Honor

Larkin Jones

After our first kindergarten play date, Mom asked if I wanted Larkin to come back to play again, and it’s a good thing moms don’t do what 5-year-olds say, because I said no. Despite my early opposition, we’ve been best friends since, surviving the agony of going to different high schools (j/k… it just meant that we had an extra homecoming to go to and two sets of friends), college apart (like in high school, this really just meant more parties and two sets of friends), and Larkin's years serving at sea, in Bahrain, and Naples. Happily, she’s back in DC (where I plans to find a way to keep her…)

Matron of Honor

Caitlin Murphy Danberg

Caitlin and I lived on the same floor freshman year of college, and we stuck like glue (or Siamese twins… which we dressed as for a costume party once). By sophomore year we worked together in the Dickinson mailroom, lived together, and had the same double major, so we shared most of our classes, too. Junior year took both of us to Toulouse, France for the year to share the challenges of living abroad and joys of traveling with the perfect travel companion (to date Caitlin and I have been to France, Spain, Italy, England, Morocco, Japan, Hungary, and Slovakia together… more to come!). Since graduation, we sadly have not been able to live together again (Jason and Caitlin’s husband seem to think that would be weird?) but luckily, she is right across the bridge in Arlington!

Bridal Party

Megan Sheahan

Megan is another Calvert County girl, and we went to middle and high school together. If there was a dance at Calvert High, chances were good that we were both in the gym pretending that overpriced cardboard decorations would bring a ridiculous theme (e.g. Whispers of the Orient, When Dreams Come True) to life. In true cool-kid form, we bonded over mandatory (but loved-by-all) “Meet the Press” watching parties, being lured into skipping school together, and the epic quantity of snow days we were rewarded with our senior year of high school. In the years since, Megan has been a valued confidant and advisor, whether living in Michigan, California, New York, or Massachusetts.

Bridesmaid

Breanna Gallagher

Breanna and I met in 2007 when we were both randomly dropped in the bustling metropolis* of Challans, France in the modern and sophisticated department of the Vendée**. I taught at the middle school and shared an apartment with the middle-aged principal, she taught at the high school and lived with a strange Mexican man. Without each other neither of us would have lasted, but thankfully we found each other and became each other’s lifeline and adventure partner. In the years since, we’ve been lucky enough to spend three years living in the same city, and now that she’s back in Seattle, Jason and I just have another reason to visit the Pacific Northwest!

* This is sarcasm, it is the opposite of a bustling metropolis
** Also sarcasm. The jokes goes, “What do you do if you want to travel back in time 50 years?” “You go to the Vendée!”

Bridesmaid

Katie Krische

As my older sister, Katie has doubtless known me the longest out of all of the bridal party. Over the years we’ve turned into a city mouse and country mouse (so says our mother), as I’ve made Washington, DC my home, and Katie has settled into farm life first in Kentucky and now Tennessee, as her childhood obsession with horses has blossomed into a career or horse breeding, livestock raising, and egg collecting. While I may wear the trappings of a city mouse, I am always delighted to have a good reason to roll in the mud with the puppies and hop on a pony in the country! She may be far away now but I love my visits to Katie and Tom in Tennessee and am thrilled she’s a part of the bridal party.
